About NIEHUB

Northern Innovation and Empowerment Hub (NIEHUB) is a social enterprise entity based and registered in Kenya as a Company Limited by Guarantee. Headquartered in Garissa, Northern Kenya, NIEHUB supports and empowers youth, girls and women in Garissa, Wajir and Mandera through ICT skills, social entrepreneurship, mentorship as well as career and job training.

NIEHUB was conceptualized in 2019 to help improve the lives of the marginalized youth, girls and women in Northern Kenya. Through well thought programs, the innovation hub offers a unique platform that equips the target group with essential life skills through professional, technological, entrepreneurial and technical initiatives. The hub provides creative working space to empower the target groups with business and soft skills so that they create economic opportunities for themselves.

According to the 2018 Kenya National Bureau of Statistics (KNBS), the unemployment rate among the youth is 70 percent which translates to seven million. Counties in Northern Kenya that have been marginalized since independence, rank top in the unemployment index survey. This has resulted in high poverty rates, substance abuse, disillusionment that has caused increase in depression and mental diseases as well as recruitment into extremist groups.

Access to Information and Communication Technology (ICT) infrastructure and services in Northern Kenya is among the lowest in the country. NIEHUB seeks to bridge this gap by providing a convenient platform to empower the community… The employment market for young people has changed significantly over the past two decades under the combined impacts of globalization, market liberalization and the adoption of innovative approaches.

Vision

To become the driving force behind transformative innovation and sustainable empowerment in North Eastern Kenya, unlocking the region's untapped potential and propelling its talented individuals to lead the digital revolution.

Mission

To foster a dynamic ecosystem of innovation and empowerment, where the untapped talent of North Eastern Kenya is nurtured, amplified, and equipped with digital skills. Through our diverse programs, partnerships, and resources, we aim to inspire, educate, and connect individuals, enabling them to thrive as innovative leaders and agents of change in the digital age.

The Founders

Fatuma A. Dubow

Fatuma A. Dubow has a wide experience in community development, having worked with the marginalized North-Eastern communities in Kenya. She is a development specialist with focus on community mobilization, community development, and NGO (Non-governmental organization) partnerships. Her experience as a Monitoring, Evaluation, Accountability and Learning (MEAL) expert puts the organization at a strategic position of efficiently using and controlling the available resources. In addition, Dubow has a Master of Arts in Development Studies from the Catholic University of East Africa. She is in charge of Projects Implementation, Monitoring and Learning component of NIEHUB.

Alinoor R. Hassan

Alinoor Hassan is an Economic development professional experienced in SME, trade and private sector including business start-ups. Previously managed large economic development projects funded by USAID, DFID and World Bank. He has over 10 years of experience in the field of research, particularly conducting qualitative and quantitative research. He has an excellence facilitating and training skills. He is experienced in working with civil organization, government agencies and international humanitarian organization. He holds a Master of Arts in Project Planning and Management from University of Nairobi. Alinoor Hassan serves as the Strategic Planning and Finance Manager for NIEHUB.

Alinoor M. Bosh

Alinoor Moulid Bosh is an experienced independent journalist, having worked in the Horn of Africa, particularly in Somalia, Kenya and Ethiopia. His specialties are in journalism, editing and online (digital) journalism, making his experience invaluable in implementing the programs of NIEHUB. He is an indispensable asset to the organization as he has traversed the north eastern region of Kenya, with strong grassroots connections in the major towns in the region. He is a graduate of Bachelor of Arts (B.A.) in Communication, Journalism, and Related Programs from the Kenya Methodist University in 2015 and a continuing student of a Master of Arts in Diplomacy and International Relations at Kenyatta University. He serves as the Operations Manager as well as the Communication person at the organization.
